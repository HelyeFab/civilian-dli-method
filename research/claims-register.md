# Claims Register

This register prevents uncertain or repeated statements from quietly becoming accepted as fact.

## Status definitions

| Status | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Verified | Directly supported by a primary or authoritative source. |
| Strongly supported | Supported by multiple credible and substantially independent sources. |
| Anecdotal | Reported through personal experience but not independently established. |
| Inferred | A reasoned conclusion derived from evidence rather than directly stated. |
| Unverified | Currently unsupported, inadequately sourced, or still under investigation. |
| Disputed | Credible evidence or testimony points in materially different directions. |

## Entry rules

1. Give every material claim a stable ID.
2. Record the narrowest defensible scope and period.
3. Link evidence notes rather than merely listing a homepage.
4. Preserve caveats and conflicting accounts.
5. Change status only when the cited evidence justifies it.
6. Record the review date whenever a claim is revised.
