# Primary Source Catalogue

**Last reviewed:** 19 July 2026

This catalogue records sources issued by DLIFLC, the U.S. government, or the Interagency Language Roundtable. Inclusion does not mean every statement in a source is accepted without qualification.

## A. Institutional structure and programme facts

### DLIFLC — About

- URL: https://www.dliflc.edu/about/
- Institution: Defense Language Institute Foreign Language Center
- Source type: official institutional overview
- Current value: documents five instructional days per week, seven classroom hours per day, two to three hours of nightly homework, course lengths of 36–64 weeks, student eligibility, approximate instructor numbers and off-site immersion design.
- Use for: operational facts and stated programme design.
- Caution: contains promotional framing; does not document the detailed internal sequence of a normal lesson.

### DLIFLC — Academics

- URL: https://www.dliflc.edu/academics/
- Source type: official academic organisation page
- Current value: identifies schools, departments, deans, chairs, instructor teams, curriculum responsibilities and continuing education.
- Use for: institutional map and distribution of responsibility.
- Caution: does not establish how consistently practices are enacted across departments.

### DLIFLC — Language Schools

- URL: https://www.dliflc.edu/academics/language-schools/
- Source type: official programme descriptions
- Current value: describes the stated whole-person, culturally integrated and student-centred approach; includes school-specific examples of immersion, operational language, cultural studies, advising and learner support.
- Use for: generating bounded claims about named schools and identifying mechanisms for further investigation.
- Caution: school descriptions are partly promotional and cannot alone establish institute-wide uniformity.

### DLIFLC — Languages Offered

- URL: https://www.dliflc.edu/about/languages-at-dliflc/
- Source type: official current programme list
- Current value: identifies the published language categories and course lengths: Category I/II at 36 weeks, Category III at 48 weeks and Category IV at 64 weeks; Japanese is listed as Category IV.
- Use for: current programme length and language-category claims.
- Caution: offerings and categories can change; retain review date.

### DLIFLC — Student Life

- URL: https://www.dliflc.edu/about/student-life/
- Source type: official student overview and handbook gateway
- Current value: confirms seven classroom hours per day, five days per week, two to three hours of homework, military physical training and joint-service demands.
- Use for: workload and institutional-context claims.
- Caution: investigate the linked student handbook separately for detailed policies.

### DLIFLC — Degrees and Registrar

- URLs:
  - https://www.dliflc.edu/administration/registrar/degrees/
  - https://www.dliflc.edu/administration/registrar/
- Source type: official academic-record and degree pages
- Current value: documents degree structure, accreditation-related material, resident-course requirements and institutional history.
- Use for: academic status and formal programme architecture.
- Caution: degree requirements are not direct evidence of daily pedagogy.

## B. Faculty standards and instructional quality

### “Teaching the DLI way: Faculty Development Support”

- URL: https://www.dliflc.edu/teaching-the-dli-way-faculty-development-support/
- Publisher: DLIFLC
- Date: 2 April 2024
- Source type: official institutional news feature with named faculty-development leadership
- Current value: reports that new instructors complete a two-week Common Faculty Development Instructor Course followed by a three-week DLIFLC Instructor Certification Course before classroom teaching.
- Use for: documented faculty-entry training and the existence of a standardisation process.
- Caution: the article gives limited detail on course content and should be followed by training documents or interviews.

### Office of Standardization and Academic Excellence

- URL: https://www.dliflc.edu/academics/osae/
- Source type: official organisational description
- Current value: documents institute-level responsibility for academic leadership, quality assurance, research, data analysis and standardisation of classroom and support practices.
- Use for: quality-control architecture.
- Caution: existence of an office does not prove uniform implementation.

## C. DLIFLC refereed and professional publications

### Dialog on Language Instruction — Journal home

- URL: https://www.dliflc.edu/ojs/dialog
- Publisher: DLIFLC
- Source type: refereed professional journal
- Current value: provides DLIFLC-authored research, action research, faculty reflections and practical teaching reports. It is one of the best public sources for classroom-level evidence.
- Use for: bounded evidence about specific interventions and recurring pedagogical concerns.
- Caution: published practices may be experimental, recommended or local rather than institute-wide.

### Dialog on Language Instruction — About

- URL: https://www.dliflc.edu/ojs/dialog/about
- Current value: states the journal’s role, review status and requirement that articles have a theoretical base and practical application.
- Use for: evaluating publication type and authority.

### Dialog archives

- URL: https://www.dliflc.edu/ojs/dialog/issue/archive
- Current value: searchable source for historical and current classroom research.
- Initial high-priority themes:

  - grammar instruction;
  - vocabulary strategies;
  - bottom-up listening;
  - reading fluency;
  - recycling and review;
  - immersion;
  - Special Assistance;
  - faculty development;
  - artificial-intelligence personalisation.

### “Process-Based and Learner-Centered Grammar Instruction”

- URL: https://www.dliflc.edu/ojs/dialog/article/view/21
- Author: Yanmei Liu
- Publication: *Dialog on Language Instruction*, Vol. 35 No. 1
- Source type: DLIFLC faculty article
- Current value: proposes four phases of grammar learning—comprehension, memorisation, internalisation and application—with phase-specific instructional strategies.
- Use for: a documented DLIFLC faculty model of grammar instruction.
- Scope: pedagogical proposal and reported implementation context; not yet institute-wide practice.

### “Improving Vocabulary Retention by Training Students to Use Vocabulary Learning Strategies In and Out of Class”

- Issue URL: https://www.dliflc.edu/ojs/dialog/issue/view/5
- Authors: Tetiana Foster and Tatiana Saenko
- Source type: action research
- Current value: evidence that explicit vocabulary-strategy training has been implemented and studied in a DLIFLC context.
- Use for: vocabulary-mechanism reconstruction.
- Next action: download and analyse the full article, including participants, intervention length, activities and outcomes.

### “Enhancing Classroom Understanding through Concept Checking Questions and Instruction Checking Questions”

- URL: https://www.dliflc.edu/ojs/dialog/article/view/29
- Author: Suyi Liu
- Source type: practical faculty article
- Current value: documents the use of concept-checking and instruction-checking questions for grammar, vocabulary and activity directions.
- Use for: lesson architecture and comprehension checking.
- Scope: recommended practice, not proof of universal use.

### “Using Microsoft’s Immersive Reader Tool to Enhance Students’ Listening Comprehension”

- URL: https://www.dliflc.edu/ojs/dialog/article/view/27

## D. Public DLIFLC learning products

### DLIFLC eLearning

- URL: https://www.dliflc.edu/elearning/
- Source type: official public courseware portal
- Current value: provides beginner, deployment, culture, advanced and assessment resources, including Headstart2, Rapport, cultural materials and GLOSS.
- Use for: direct analysis of publicly visible activity design, task progression, audio/text integration and cultural content.
- Caution: short deployment courses and self-study products are not equivalent to the resident basic course.

### GLOSS — Global Language Online Support System

- Accessed through: https://www.dliflc.edu/elearning/
- Source type: official public lesson system
- Current value: thousands of intermediate and advanced reading/listening lessons across languages and topic areas.
- Use for: artefact-based analysis of lesson stages, proficiency targeting and assessment items.
- Next action: sample lessons across Japanese and at least two other language categories.

### Headstart2

- Accessed through: https://www.dliflc.edu/elearning/
- Source type: official self-paced introductory product
- Current value: approximately 100 hours organised around Sound & Script and Military Tasks.
- Use for: analysing opening-stage phonology/script design and task-based vocabulary.
- Caution: designed for deployment preparation, not as a miniature resident DLI course.

## E. Proficiency and assessment framework

### Interagency Language Roundtable — Homepage and Skill Level Descriptions

- URLs:
  - https://govtilr.org/
  - https://www.govtilr.org/Skills/Listening.htm
  - https://www.govtilr.org/Skills/Speaking.htm
- Source type: official U.S. government interagency proficiency framework
- Current value: defines functional proficiency from level 0 to 5, including plus levels, across listening, speaking, reading and writing.
- Use for: decoding outcome targets, proficiency-oriented curriculum and assessment language.
- Caution: the ILR scale defines proficiency; it does not prescribe one teaching method.

### ILR Competence in Intercultural Communication

- URL: https://www.govtilr.org/Skills/Competence.htm
- Source type: official skill-level description
- Current value: defines intercultural competence as effective participation through appropriate language and behaviour in social and professional contexts.
- Use for: analysing DLI’s claim that language and culture are integrated.
