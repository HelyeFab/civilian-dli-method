# Project Roadmap

## Phase 1 — Establish the evidence base

- Define the research questions.
- Create a source-evaluation framework.
- Catalogue official DLIFLC and U.S. government material.
- Catalogue academic research on intensive language programmes.
- Record graduate and instructor testimony separately from institutional evidence.
- Build and maintain the claims register.

## Phase 2 — Reconstruct the instructional system

Investigate and document:

- programme length and language categories;
- the daily and weekly schedule;
- lesson sequencing;
- vocabulary introduction and recycling;
- grammar instruction;
- listening progression;
- speaking drills and interaction patterns;
- reading and writing development;
- homework, testing, remediation, and attrition;
- immersion events and cultural instruction;
- proficiency targets and ILR assessment.

## Phase 3 — Explain why the system works

- Compare observed practices with second-language acquisition research.
- Identify high-value mechanisms such as retrieval, spacing, feedback, time-on-task, and integrated skills.
- Separate useful pedagogy from military-specific constraints.
- Identify likely costs, trade-offs, and failure modes.

## Phase 4 — Design the civilian method

Create realistic versions for:

- 60–90 minutes per day;
- 2–3 hours per day;
- an intensive full-time programme;
- individual learners;
- tutor-supported learners;
- AI-assisted learners.

## Phase 5 — Japanese adaptation

Add explicit systems for:

- kana automation;
- kanji acquisition and reading;
- vocabulary in chunks and collocations;
- politeness and register;
- pitch-accent awareness;
- listening segmentation;
- natural spoken contractions;
- JLPT alignment without reducing the programme to test preparation.

## Phase 6 — Pilot and evaluation

- Build sample study weeks and lesson units.
- Define measurable outcomes.
- Run learner experiments.
- Record adherence, workload, retention, and speaking performance.
- Revise the framework based on evidence.
